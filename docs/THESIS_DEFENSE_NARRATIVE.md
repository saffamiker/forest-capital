## The Human Judgment Question

If a reviewer asks **"where is the human analytical judgment given
you have an AI council and a platform?"** the prepared response is:

> The platform executes. The judgment is in what we built it to do
> and why.
>
> Five decisions required human judgment that the AI council could
> not have made:
>
> **One.** Interpreting the 2022 correlation shift as structural
> rather than cyclical. The data shows a shift. The interpretation —
> that it changes the nature of the portfolio construction problem —
> is ours.
>
> **Two.** Choosing to span ten distinct signal mechanisms rather
> than optimise parameters on one approach. We could have run five
> hundred variations of momentum. We chose to ask whether different
> mechanisms produce different results. That is a research design
> decision.
>
> **Three.** Disclosing that no strategy achieves statistical
> significance, when we could have reported Sharpe rankings and
> moved on. We chose honesty about the limits of the evidence.
>
> **Four.** Designing dissent into the AI council. We could have
> asked the AI for a recommendation and taken it. We built a
> structure that forces the system to challenge itself before it
> advises us.
>
> **Five.** The constraint framework. Forty percent maximum per
> strategy and fifty percent maximum per asset class are not
> data-derived. They are fiduciary design decisions about what an
> institutional mandate can implement responsibly.
>
> The platform gives us the evidence. The interpretation, the
> design, and the governance framework are ours.

### Delivery notes

- Deliver verbatim when the question is asked in that form.
- If a follow-up zooms in on one of the five, expand on that one but
  always return to "the platform executes, the judgment is ours."
- Tone: collegial, not defensive. The reviewer is asking a real
  question; the answer is a real position.
